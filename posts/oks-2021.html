I am that guy now.

The OND quarter of books.

At the end of October this year, I resolved to read a book per week. I am glad that I have exceeded my expectations, having read 18 books since then. In this journey so far, I have discovered a couple of things * I love reading, more than I thought. * Number of books per unit time is a bad metric, if it gets in the way of me picking up heavier books. I should just make sure, I always have something to read.

The stats roundup.

  • Pages consumed: ~5600
  • Books read: 18
  • Longest book: ~600 pages (Thinking, fast and slow.)
  • Shortest book: ~110 pages (Sum: Forty tales of afterlives)
  • Average book length: ~310 pages

Year end list (in no particular order)

I am going to read fiction, and non-fiction. But considering I mostly read non-fiction, the list will be biased towards it. I am adding an arbitrary number of books, that have stuck with me. * Deep Work * Atomic Habits * Sapiens * The Psychology of Money * The Almanack of Naval Ravikant * The Millionaire Fastlane * Livewired * Why we sleep? * Sum: Forty tales of afterlives * Thinking, fast and slow.

Books I particularly disliked, and will actively hate on.

  • A Brief History of Time
  • 50 shades of Grey.
